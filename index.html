Technical Guides for End Users

How to Create a Strong Password

Ensure your account security with a robust password.

  • Length is Key: Aim for at least 12 characters. More characters equal more security.
  • Mix it Up: Use a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid Common Words: Steer clear of complete words, easily guessable phrases, or sequences (like "password" or "1234").
  • Use Phrases: Think of a random phrase and use the first letter of each word, mixing it with numbers and symbols.
  • Consider a Password Manager: These tools can generate and store complex passwords for you.
  • Change Regularly: Update your passwords periodically, especially if you suspect they may have been compromised.

Additional Tips:

  • Do not reuse passwords across multiple sites.
  • Avoid using personal information such as birthdays or names.

How to End a Task in Task Manager

Close unresponsive or unnecessary applications on your Windows PC.

  • Open Task Manager: Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc or right-click the taskbar and select "Task Manager."
  • Find the Application: Look through the list of applications and background processes. If you know the name of the application you want to close, find it in the list.
  • End the Task: Click on the application or process, then click "End Task" at the bottom-right of the window.

Note:

Be cautious when ending processes. Ending essential system processes may cause your computer to malfunction or shut down.
